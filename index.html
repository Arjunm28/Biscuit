How toy poodles learn

Toy poodles rank among the top 3 most intelligent dog breeds. They thrive on mental stimulation and can become bored — and naughty — without it. Key points:

  • They respond best to positive reinforcement — never punishment
  • Short sessions (3–5 min at this age) beat long ones
  • They pick up patterns fast — vary your rewards to keep engagement high
  • Their sensitive nature means a harsh "no" can shut them down for hours
  • Use their nose — scent games count as mental exercise
🍖

Reward science

Use three tiers of reward based on difficulty:

  • Tier 1 (known skills): Kibble, verbal praise, petting
  • Tier 2 (in progress): Small soft treats (chicken, cheese)
  • Tier 3 (new or hard): Jackpot — several small treats in quick succession + big celebration

Keep treats tiny (pea-sized) — Biscuit is small and you'll be treating a lot!

🌍

Socialisation window — act now

The critical socialisation window closes at about 16 weeks. Right now is the single most important time in Biscuit's life. Aim to positively expose him to:

  • 20+ different types of people (hats, uniforms, children, beards)
  • Other vaccinated dogs and animals
  • Surfaces: grass, gravel, tiles, metal, carpet, sand
  • Sounds: traffic, thunder, vacuum, fireworks (low volume)
  • Handling: ears, paws, mouth, tail — prep for grooming and vet visits
✂️

Grooming prep (start now!)

Toy poodles need regular professional grooming every 6–8 weeks for life. Starting early is critical:

  • Touch his paws, ears and mouth daily — reward calmly
  • Introduce a brush this week — one stroke, treat, done
  • Book a puppy "intro groom" at 12–14 weeks just for a bath and blow-dry
  • The more positive early experiences, the easier grooming will be forever
😴

Sleep & rest

Puppies need 16–20 hours of sleep per day. Over-stimulation is a real issue — a tired puppy bites more, focuses less and regresses on training. Biscuit already settles in his pen and crate, which is exceptional. Protect his rest times and never skip naps for extra training.
